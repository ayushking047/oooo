Vowels 5 letters

Vowels are made with an open mouth and no blockage from your tongue, teeth, or lips. Every English word needs at least one — they're the sound that carries a syllable.

Try it: say "cat", "bed", "sit", "top", "sun" slowly — feel how the vowel is the open, held sound in the middle.

Consonants 21 letters

Consonants are made by partly or fully blocking airflow with your lips, teeth, or tongue — like the snap of "b", the hiss of "s", or the hum of "m".

Try it: in "bright", the vowel "i" is surrounded by four consonants — b, r, g, h, t all shape the sound around it.
